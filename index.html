My Method of Teaching

My lessons typically consist of the following structure:

  • First, I recap the topics from the previous lesson and link it into the current lesson, to allow a sense of continuity.
  • I then teach the current topic, using plenty of examples to embed understanding.
  • We go through some brief exercises to then reinforce that understanding.
  • I set some (optional!) homework to strengthen the learning, and so that the lessons don’t get too bogged down with too many exercises.

I also use the following teaching techniques (depending on which work best for each student):

  • I always go at the student’s pace, checking in every now and again to make sure they understand everything.
  • I never teach at the student, I always teach to the student: by not just stating facts they need to memorise, but by carefully explaining the content.
  • I provide exercises during the class and as homework to deepen understanding and cement topics further, especially since doing something can be a much better way to learn than just hearing about it.
  • I make lessons fun and engaging by keeping them light and varied, and by showing their relevancy in the world.
  • I use visual examples to emphasise what I am explaining.
