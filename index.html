My Method of Teaching

In my time as a student, I have had many great, and many awful teachers, and I try to learn lessons from both to improve my technique:

  • I recap old topics often to reinforce them into the student’s understanding.
  • I do my best not just to tell the student about a topic, but to explain it clearly, since this makes it easier to remember as opposed to disjointed facts.
  • I provide exercises during the class and as homework to deepen understanding and cement topics further, especially since doing something can be a much better way to learn than just hearing about it.
  • I make lessons fun and engaging by keeping them light and varied, and by showing their relevancy in the world.
  • I use visual examples to emphasise what I am explaining.

Not all of these methods will work on every student; everybody is unique. Therefore, over the first few lessons, I aim to learn which of these methods works best for each student in order to achieve the best learning experience for future lessons.
